M'Shewsha

Description

M'Shewsha is a traditional Algerian dish often enjoyed for breakfast or served with coffee. It features a base of beaten eggs mixed with semolina and a touch of flour, fried until fluffy and slightly golden. Warm honey is then poured over it, allowing it to soak in and create a rich, sweet-savory treat [[4]].

Ingredients

  • Eggs (4â€“6)
  • Fine semolina (2 tablespoons)
  • All-purpose flour (1 tablespoon)
  • Sugar (optional, for extra sweetness)
  • Butter or oil (for frying)
  • Honey (to drizzle generously)
  • Cinnamon (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, beat the eggs well until slightly frothy.
  2. Add semolina and flour, and mix into a smooth batter.
  3. Heat butter or oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  4. Pour small amounts of batter to form pancake-like shapes.
  5. Cook each side until lightly golden and cooked through.
  6. Transfer to serving plates while warm.
  7. Pour warm honey generously over the top, letting it soak in before serving.
  8. Garnish with cinnamon if desired.
